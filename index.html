July 30th- August 6th 2017

International Youth Conference on Climate Change 2017 (IYCCC)

International Youth Conference on Climate Change 2017 (IYCCC) is the ultimate conference that will take place in an environment that favours mutual learning, networking, sight-seeing and entertainment while exposing young audiences from all over the world to the most pressing issues around the theme of Climate Change, surrounded by the beautiful landscape in Sogn og Fjordane, Western Norway. The aim of the conference is cultural, political and exchange of knowledge on global climate change issues.

About Us

The conference shall be designed in two sets:
1- From 10-17.
2- Audiences aged 18 and above.
Providing a platform a to discuss global issues on an annual basis giving them a chance to participate in educative and cultural exchange goals at national and international levels.

IYCCC collaborates with different partners regionally, nationally and internationally including institutions of learning in order to broaden a shared experience. All activities shall focus on the conference theme with an opportunity to visit various organizations and on site activities in order to gain first hand knowledge rounded up by excursions and career fairs.

The conference aims at achieving;

  • Cultural exchanges, between Norwegian, foreign students and professors.
  • Workshops with resolution papers and projects that can be implemented in different countries.
  • Recruiting sessions to bridge the gap between students/graduates and job opportunities at the international level
  • Study abroad exchanges
  • Tourism promotion of the Sogn og Fjordane region.
  • Career fairs
  • Sports and entertainment

Workshops

The workshops will be held at different locations (kommunes) within Sogn region. Click on the "Show" link below to read more about each workshop:

  1. Effects of carbon emissions on climate change.
    2. Show

    There seems no doubt that carbon dioxide pollution is increasing to levels never experienced before, mainly through burning of fossil fuels which is not only in reference to oil and gas but also coal/peat/manure in developing countries. For instance, there has been widespread burning of forests to create palm oil plantantations through slash and burn in some countries. This increase in CO2 levels is undoubtedly directly responsible for increasing global temperatures with industrial sectors in the western world also playing a major role in carbon emissions. On the other hand an open source movement (opening climate data by industrial companies to the public) seems to be one step closer to tackling this major problem, but how feasible is it?

  2. Climate change and technological innovations: geo-engineering
    3. Show

    The belief that climate change can be halted by use of man made means has led to controversies. For instance, the use of algae farms, releasing volcanic ash as coolants, capturing emissions from fuel, arranging mirrors in space to redirect solar rays, painting roofs white instead of black, using meat replacements such as encouraging chicken instead of beef given that more land is needed to produce beef than chicken and more power is needed to prepare one burger which is equivalent to fully charging 7 ipads just to mention but a few have been suggested as means to curb the rise of temperatures but what are the health and environmental effects of these methods?

  3. Climate change impact on biodiversity and human life
    4. Show

    The temperature related effects of climate change have seemed to threaten the quality of the life we live. Rising sea levels, extreme weather, warming and cooling temperatures affect the weather we experience, the food we eat, water we drink and air we breathe. These effects are heightened by individual's location or where he or she lives, their exposure and sensitivity to these effects, as well as the community or nations role in developing adaptable measures. Even though these impacts have a great potential of affecting biodiversity and human health, what measures are put in place by communities, individuals and governments to adapt and prepare for these changes?

  4. Climate change and Migration not Migtation
    5. Show

    Human displacement is a complex of mix of many factors. Whereas climate change is seen as a driver of human migration, the question begs, has many wars and migrations across the world initially caused by years of drought and reduced food supplies led to large scale human migrations?

  5. Climate change impact on sports and recreational activities
    6. Show

    The influence of climate change on sports and recreational activities has been overlooked in many instances yet it affects a percentage of not only players and athletes but also event organisers, world economies as well as governments. For example, warm conditions in Russia impacted the Sochi Winter Games leading them to spend $50 Billion resulting in the most expensive winter Olympics with an undocumented amount of money spent on preservation of cubic yards of artificial snow. Players win by pushing their bodies against the limit and as a result, they become more susceptible to changing environments. Warm weather sports are affected as well, fot example, tennis games in Australia 2014 became such a serious issue in the sense that water bottles melted, a players shoe melted, just to mentionn but a few. Footballers also risk overheating due to heavy padding, Ice Hockey is almost at risk of becoming an endangered sport due to warming conditions. How can sports enthusiasts and professionals work in advocating for safer climate change resolutions?
