International Youth Peace Conference 2017 (IYPC)
Sept. 23rd- Sept. 30th 2017

The International Youth Peace Conference 2017 (IYPC) in collaboration with United Nations Youth Associations Network, host institutions Sogndal University College and United World College Red Cross Nordic, will be arranged within 5 communities in Western Norway for participants aged 16 and above, surrounded by the famous UNESCO fjords, glaciers, mountains and waterfalls as they rotate to the different districts for different workshops, in an environment that favors mutual learning, networking, sight-seeing and entertainment around this year’s theme of Climate Change.

About Us

The aim of the conference is to create an arena for youth from the age of 16 and above across the world to discuss cultural, political, and environmental issues related to peace. IYPC collaborates with different partners regionally, nationally and internationally including institutions of learning in order to broaden a shared experience.


Workshops

Workshops consist of a combination of seminars, lectures and hands-on activities. They may or may not be combined with the daily social events. Since participants rotate, the workshops are being repeated during the week.


Click on the "Show" link below to read more about each workshop:

  1. Climate Change and International Politics (Venue: Sogndal)
    2. Show

    The atmosphere is a global common and we need a global treaty to stop the dumping of greenhouse gas emissions into the air. Take the role of one of 15 nations, a climate activist or an industrial lobbyist to participate in the simulation game “World Climate” in order to negotiate a global treaty. See if you can do better than the world leaders. The negotiation rounds are moderated by the “UNFCCC general secretary” and scientific support is provided by the “IPCC lead scientist” (e.g. using a real-time global climate model).

    Agenda

    9.30 am Introduction & distribution of roles
    10 am - 02 pm Negotiation rounds 1, 2 & if necessary 3
    2 pm - 3 pm Negotiation rounds 1, 2 & if necessary 3
    2 pm - 3 pm Debriefing of the Game

    Partner/Organizational Support
    t.b.c

    Associate professor August Wierling

  2. Climate Change and Food (Venue: Vik)
    3. Show

    The carbon footprint of food matters a lot. In this workshop you will experience how your daily choices can lower greenhouse gas emissions enormously. Learn from food scientists and cook with famous chefs. Sneak into the environmentally-friendly cuisine and traditional farming cultures from Africa, Europe, Asia, Australia and America.

    Agenda

    10-12 am Learning about food & climate change (invited experts)
    1 pm-3 pm Cooking with the chefs

  3. Climate Change and Nature (Venue: Fjærland)
    4. Show

    Raising temperatures and sea levels will dramatically alter our planet's landscape and biosphere. Visit one of the finest museums of Norway and learn about the causes, impacts and risks associated with climate change. Watch the fragility of retreating glaciers yourself and experience the beauty of the pristine nature of Norway's fjords and mountains in a guided hike.

    Agenda

    10-12 am Visiting the Glacier Museum (Group 1),
    1 pm-3 pm Visiting the Glacier Museum (Group 2), ...

  4. Climate Change and Revolutionary Ideas (Venue: Luster)
    5. Show

    Adapting and mitigating climate change calls for creative solutions that account for geographical, cultural and political differences. We need to develop new perspectives for the people on the globe. We need to find fairer schemes of how to shoulder the burden of managing a changing climate and we need to share responsibilities for transforming our societies in a sustainable way. Get inspired from great socio-technical inventors. Learn from creativity teachers and design yourself a solution in small working groups. Let us enable the creative potential of the youth.

    Agenda

    10-11 am Seminar on creativity techniques
    11-02 pm Working in groups to design ideas and shaping solutions
    02-03 pm Bazaar with speed dating about ideas and “Escalator Pitches” (or “Waterfall pitches”).

    Partner/Organizational Support
    Interested sponsors, Luster commune (t.b.c)

  6. Climate Change and Peace (Venue: All locations)
    7. Show

    The Youth Speech and Nomination of a Youth Peace Prize
    The International Panel of Climate Change regards it as likely that climate change will lead to large displacement of people. The risk of undermining the stability of the societies – and hence peace – is all but low. Debate with politicians, representatives of NGO, and staff of agencies for official development assistance to express your ideas. Nominate your candidate for the World Youth Nobel Price for Peace which we will announce at the final event. Work on your contribution to the design of the event's peace pole which will be placed in all workshop locations as monuments for peace and as memorials for the UN Youth Conference in Western Norway.

    Further information
    Peace Pole Movement: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_pole Nobel Peace Center, Oslo: https://www.nobelpeacecenter.org/en/

    Agenda
    Half a day dedicated to speeches and nomination, half a day dedicated for practical work on peace pole design (will be officially shown at the final event and placed as a monument at a location yet to be decided, e.g. at HiSF campus).

Entertainment

Opening and closing ceremonies for the IYPC 2017 will feature concerts by international headlining acts as well as regional and national musicians. They will be open for the general public.


The concert is managed by Afrost Events. Artists line up, ticketing and more information to be announced soon.

Youth speech

Partners Conference

To attend this conference, the companies have to financially support the IYPC 2017.

TRANSPORT SECTOR
