International Youth Peace Conference 2017 (IYPC)

International Youth Peace Conference 2017 (IYPC) is the ultimate conference that will take place in an environment that favours mutual learning, networking, sight-seeing and entertainment while exposing young audiences from all over the world to the most pressing issues around this year’s theme of Climate Change within 5 districts "Kommune" in Sogn region, surrounded by the beautiful landscape in Western Norway. The aim of the conference is cultural, political and exchange of knowledge on global issues related to peace.

About Us

The conference shall be designed for two sets of age groups:

1. 10-17 years.
2. 18 years and above.

With the aim of providing a platform to discuss global issues on an annual basis giving them a chance to participate in educative and cultural exchange goals at national and international levels.

IYPC collaborates with different partners regionally, nationally and internationally including institutions of learning in order to broaden a shared experience. All activities shall focus on the conference theme with an opportunity to visit various organizations and on site activities in order to gain first hand knowledge rounded up by excursions and career fairs.

The conference aims at achieving:
- Establishing an annual (Youth Peace Prize ) winner nominated by the youth..
- Make resolutions and peace pacts that can be adapted globally.
- Model United Nations Simulations.
- Cultural exchanges, between Norwegian, foreign students, experts and professors.
- Workshops with resolution papers and projects that can be implemented in different countries.
- Recruiting sessions to bridge the gap between students/graduates and job opportunities at the national and international level.
- Study abroad exchanges.
- Tourism promotion of Norway and especially the Sogn og Fjordane region.
- Career fairs.
- Sports and entertainment.

Workshops

The workshops will be held within 5 districts on a rotational basis during the week. (Timetable shall be uploaded soon).

Click on the "Show" link below to read more about each workshop:

  1. Climate change and climate policy (Sogndal District)
    2. Show

    The workshop will provide an overview on the knowledge about the climate processes as well as politics and governance of climate change. The latter will be achieved through the study of the actors, content and policy instruments in international climate agreements. There will be a specific focus on the IPCC, and their methodology and working practices, ecosystem response to climate change, climate mitigation, climate change adaptation and how to combine climate change adaptation and mitigation strategies, and how to move from adjustment to transformation of society.

  2. Climate change and biodiversity (Leikanger District)
    3. Show

    The temperature related effects of climate change have seemed to threaten the quality of the life we live. Rising sea levels, extreme weather, warming and cooling temperatures affect the weather we experience, the food we eat, water we drink and air we breathe. This workshop will highlight the effects of climate change on humans, other species interactions and the eco system. It shall further look at the distribution in alpine habitats, invasive species, impacts on marine systems and impacts on ecosystem services such as pollination while providing relevant initiatives in natural resource management that can reduce the detrimental effects of climate change on ecosystems. Even though these impacts have a great potential of affecting the eco systems, what measures have been put in place by communities, individuals and governments to adapt and prepare for these changes?

  3. Transition towards a zero emission society (Luster District)
    4. Show

    There seems no doubt that carbon dioxide pollution is increasing to levels never experienced before, mainly through burning of fossil fuels which is not only in reference to oil and gas but also coal/peat/manure in developing countries. This increase in CO2 levels is undoubtedly directly responsible for increasing global temperatures with industrial sectors in the western world also playing a major role in carbon emissions. On the other hand an open source movement (opening climate data by industrial companies to the public) seems to be one step closer to tackling this major problem, but how feasible is it?

  4. Climate change and climate refugees (Balestrand District)
    5. Show

    Human displacement is a complex mix of many factors. Whereas climate change is seen as a driver of human migration, the question begs, has many wars and migrations across the world initially caused by years of drought and reduced food supplies led to large scale human migrations?

  5. Climate change, sports and recreational activities (Vik District )
    6. Show

    The influence of climate change on sports and recreational activities has been overlooked in many instances yet it affects a percentage of not only players and athletes but also event organizers, world economies as well as governments. For example, warm conditions in Russia impacted the Sochi Winter Games leading them to spend $50 Billion resulting in the most expensive winter Olympics with an undocumented amount of money spent on preservation of cubic yards of artificial snow. Warm weather sports are affected as well, for example, tennis games in Australia 2014 became such a serious issue in the sense that water bottles and a player's shoe melted. Footballers also risk overheating due to heavy padding, Ice Hockey is almost at risk of becoming an endangered sport due to warming conditions just to mention but a few. The question raised is, how can sports enthusiasts and professionals work in advocating for safer climate change resolutions?

  6. Sustainable development and societal transformation (Sogndal District)
    7. Show

    While looking into relevant scientific problems and methods put in place to curb climate change, the belief that these methods can be halted by use of man-made means has led to controversies. Information Technological advances by IT companies, media’s role in enlightening the public as well as measures put forward by Industries for instance, the use of algae farms, releasing volcanic ash as coolants, capturing emissions from fuel, arranging mirrors in space to redirect solar rays, painting roofs white instead of black, using meat replacements such as encouraging chicken instead of beef just to mention but a few have been suggested as means to tackle the issue of climate but to what extent are these entities effective?

Projects

Our goal is to create a long standing tradition of nurturing future leaders by encouraging delegates to develop projects before hand that can be introduced at the conference with the best, most applicable project winning grants for implementation in the society.

Guidelines
-Delegates can develop projects individually or as a group.
-Project must address a need within the theme of Climate change.
-It should be realistic to achieve and maintain.
For more information, kindly contact projects@internationalconferences.no

Peace Price

We would like to recognize the youth who are doing an outstanding job within their countries and communities. The goal of the conference is to set up a Youth Peace prize. Companies are more than welcome to sponsor the youth prize.
